LATEST GLOBAL NEWS HEADLINES - JUNE 16, 2026

1. UNITED STATES-IRAN PEACE AGREEMENT
--------------------------------------
President Donald Trump presented the preliminary U.S.-Iran agreement at the G-7 summit in Evian-les-Bains, France. The deal, which aims to end the recent conflict in the Gulf, outlines a 60-day ceasefire and includes plans to reopen the strategic Strait of Hormuz to normal shipping transit. G-7 allies remain cautious as many specifics of the agreement are still undisclosed. A formal signing ceremony is scheduled to take place on June 19 in Geneva, Switzerland.

2. U.S. AIR FORCE B-52 BOMBER CRASH
------------------------------------
A U.S. Air Force B-52 Stratofortress bomber crashed during a routine test flight in California. Military officials at Edwards Air Force Base confirmed the crash, stating that all eight crew members aboard the aircraft are believed to have died. Investigations into the cause of the crash are currently underway.

3. UNITED KINGDOM BAN ON SOCIAL MEDIA FOR UNDER-16s
----------------------------------------------------
British Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced a comprehensive ban on social media access for children under the age of 16 in the United Kingdom. Starmer defended the decision as the "right choice" for the government to protect children's mental health and safety online.

4. NEW EU SANCTIONS PACKAGE TARGETING RUSSIA
---------------------------------------------
The European Union officially adopted a new round of sanctions targeting 34 individuals and 47 entities. The measures specifically target Russia's "shadow fleet" of tankers, its military-industrial complex, and energy revenue channels in an effort to tighten existing economic restrictions.

5. GLOBAL SHIFT IN MEDIA CONSUMPTION
-------------------------------------
A milestone report by the Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism revealed that social media and online video platforms have officially surpassed traditional television and newspapers as the primary news sources for the global population in 2026.
